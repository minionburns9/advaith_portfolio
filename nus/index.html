My story

Mathematics became my point of interest as the best questions have, time and again, not shown the answer immediately. My first competitive exam was the Raffles Mathematical Olympiad 2024, where I received a Certificate of Distinction in Round 1 Junior Category. That experience made me more curious about challenging Math problems and encouraged me to continue trying other competitions.

I try one method, check if it works, and sometimes need to start again, especially in cryptarithms. Competitions have taught me several times that being stuck is part of learning and writing down my thoughts is crucial to solve questions quickly and accurately.

Over the last few years, I have joined many Math and Science competitions that helped me practise patience, accuracy, and creative thinking. I am proud of my awards, but I also know that the most important part is how I improve and learn after each competition. It shows my dedication towards seeking knowledge and striving for better results and awards.

How I Learn

What my achievements have taught me

When I am stuck

I try to redraw the problem, test smaller cases, look for patterns, or explain the question in my own words before trying again.

When I make mistakes

I check whether the mistake came from rushing, misunderstanding the question, or using the wrong strategy. This helps me improve my next attempt.

When I learn with others

I want to become better at explaining my thinking, listening to other methods, and learning how different students approach the same question.
